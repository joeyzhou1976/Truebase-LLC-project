## Style Prompt

TrueBase opening film should feel quiet, cinematic, and deeply personal: a living memory of a corgi and its owner, held in warm daylight and soft garden shadows. The film uses real photography, restrained typography, slow focus-pull transitions, and gentle memorial-product cues to express care, companionship, remembrance, and the transformation of daily moments into lasting keepsakes.

## Colors

- `#F7F1E7` — warm ivory for primary text and quiet light.
- `#1E2A24` — deep garden charcoal for overlays and logo contrast.
- `#8C6A3D` — aged brass for memory lines and small accents.
- `#D9B98F` — soft tan for glowing highlights.
- `#557061` — moss green for calm brand depth.

## Typography

- Display: `Fraunces`, used for large emotional statements and the closing brand line.
- Supporting text: `IBM Plex Sans`, used for small captions, service language, and quiet business context.

## Motion Rules

- Use slow Ken Burns movement on photographs, never fast zooms.
- Scene changes should use blur crossfades and warm light washes.
- Text appears like a memory surfacing: low opacity, slight upward drift, soft focus to crisp.
- Keep the final TrueBase mark stable and centered; the ending should feel resolved, not promotional.

## What NOT to Do

- No stock-like abstract backgrounds, loud gradients, or floating decorative orbs.
- No hard jump cuts between memories.
- No aggressive sales copy or crowded product catalog layout.
- No cartoon pet graphics; the supplied dog photos are the emotional anchor.
